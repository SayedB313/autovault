## Idea Summary

High-end car storage is a growing industry with no purpose-built software. Facilities track climate conditions by hand. Owner updates go out by text. Detailing and transport get scheduled over the phone. The cars sitting in these buildings are worth tens of millions. The operations holding it all together were never designed for that kind of value.

Prova gives facility operators a single dashboard for every car in the building. Each vehicle has a profile: climate logs, service history, photos, owner contact, insurance status. Owners get a portal where they can see their car's condition, schedule detailing or maintenance, and book transport. Every service request runs through a vetted marketplace of detailers, mechanics, and transport providers, with Prova taking 10 to 20 percent on each job.

The operator dashboard is the first build: vehicle intake forms, climate sensor integrations, and automated scheduling logic. Three facilities running manually is enough to refine the workflows before building the owner-facing portal. The owner portal is the selling point but the operator dashboard is what keeps the facility running daily. The marketplace layer comes last because it requires enough facility volume to attract quality service providers. Getting the operations right unlocks everything else.

Facilities pay $500 to $1,500 a month depending on size. Churn is near zero once a facility runs operations through the system. Vehicle histories, climate logs, and service records accumulate over years. That documented provenance follows the car through its life and adds resale value that owners and auction houses will eventually pay to access. The sales cycle is slow but the pitch is simple: the cars in the building are worth more than most real estate portfolios, and the management software should reflect that.
