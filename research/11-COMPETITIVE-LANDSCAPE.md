# PROVA - Competitive Landscape

---

## Direct Competitor

### Storeganise
- **Type:** General self-storage management software
- **Gap:** Lacks vehicle-specific features (climate logging, vehicle profiles, provenance tracking, detailing marketplace)
- **Positioning:** Built for generic self-storage — lockers, units, warehouse space
- **Why it's not a threat:** Not designed for the luxury segment. No owner portal, no service marketplace, no vehicle-specific workflows

---

## Indirect Competitors

### Manual Operations (The Real Competitor)
- **How facilities currently operate:**
  - Climate tracking: handwritten logs or basic HVAC readouts
  - Owner communication: text messages, phone calls, email
  - Service scheduling: phone calls to detailers, mechanics, transport
  - Vehicle records: spreadsheets, paper files, or nothing
- **Why they persist:** Low upfront cost, no learning curve, "it works well enough"
- **Weakness:** Error-prone, unscalable, no data accumulation, no owner visibility

### General Property Management Software
- Tools like Buildium, AppFolio, or Yardi manage real estate properties
- Not designed for vehicle-specific operations
- Could theoretically be adapted but would require significant customization

### Automotive Dealership Management Systems (DMS)
- Tools like CDK Global, DealerSocket, Reynolds & Reynolds
- Designed for sales operations, not storage
- Overkill for storage facility needs, wrong feature set

## Competitive Moat

1. **Data Moat** — Years of accumulated climate logs, service history, and provenance records become irreplaceable
2. **Network Effects** — More facilities = more service providers on marketplace = better experience for owners
3. **Switching Costs** — Once operational data is embedded, migrating away destroys documented vehicle history
4. **First-Mover Advantage** — Blue ocean with no direct competitors; first to establish becomes the standard
5. **Marketplace Lock-In** — Vetted service providers (detailers, mechanics, transport) create two-sided network effects
